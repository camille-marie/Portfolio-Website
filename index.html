About Me

I'm a classically-trained cellist and audio engineer who has discovered the artistry in the space between technical precision and human expression. Through national and international competitions, including semi-finalist recognition at the Fischoff National Chamber Music Competition, and collaborations with Grammy-winning ensembles like the Lincoln Trio and Black Oak Ensemble, I've learned that excellence emerges from the ability to translate complex ideas through collaborative performance.

My journey led me to Berklee College of Music, where I pursued Electronic Production & Design, merging my musical expertise with technological innovation. I've performed original works like "White Space" at both the MIT Spatial Sound Lab and Boston Conservatory's surround dome stage, experiences that confirmed my passion for spatial audio and its potential to create more inclusive musical experiences.
